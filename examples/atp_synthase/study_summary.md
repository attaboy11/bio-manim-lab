# ATP synthase and oxidative phosphorylation — summary

Cells make almost all their ATP by pumping protons across a membrane and then
letting them fall back through a tiny rotary machine. The energy is
transduced twice: first from electrons into a proton gradient, then from
that gradient into a chemical bond.

## Mechanism in steps

1. NADH delivers high-energy electrons to Complex I of the electron transport
   chain in the inner mitochondrial membrane.
2. Complexes I, III and IV use the energy released as electrons are passed
   down the chain to pump protons from the matrix into the intermembrane
   space.
3. The inner membrane is impermeable to protons, so they accumulate. The
   result is a combined chemical (pH) and electrical (membrane potential)
   gradient — the *proton-motive force*.
4. The only legal way back across the membrane is through ATP synthase. Its
   F0 ring sits in the membrane.
5. Each proton crossing F0 rotates the c-ring by one step. A central stalk
   rigidly connected to the rotor turns with it.
6. Rotation of the stalk forces the three catalytic β-subunits of F1 through
   open, loose and tight states (the binding-change mechanism). ADP and Pi
   bind in loose, are squeezed into ATP in tight, and ATP is released from
   open.
7. Oxygen at Complex IV accepts the spent electrons. Without it, the chain
   backs up, the gradient collapses, and ATP synthesis stops.

## Why it matters

Oxidative phosphorylation produces about 90% of the ATP your body uses every
day. Drugs and poisons that target any single piece — cyanide on Complex IV,
DNP as an uncoupler, mitochondrial diseases on the ETC — cause symptoms
within minutes to hours.

## Watch out for

- ATP synthase does **not** make ATP from electrons. Electrons never reach
  it. The ETC and ATP synthase are coupled only by the proton gradient.
- The proton-motive force is **not** just a pH difference — the electrical
  component usually does most of the work in mammalian mitochondria.
- The H⁺ : ATP ratio is **not** 1 : 1. It is roughly 8 : 3 (varies with
  c-ring size).
- The membrane's impermeability is doing real work. Punch a hole in it and
  the whole system stops, even if every protein still functions.

## Key takeaways

- Energy is transduced twice: electrons → gradient → chemical bond.
- ATP synthase is a literal rotary machine.
- Membrane impermeability is a load-bearing assumption.
- O₂ is upstream of ATP synthase, not downstream.
- The same logic — ion gradient drives mechanical work — appears all over
  cell biology.
