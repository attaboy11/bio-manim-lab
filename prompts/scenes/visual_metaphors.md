# Visual metaphors

A short reference for the metaphors the scene planner is allowed to use.
The point of restricting metaphors is consistency: the same visual idea must
mean the same thing across every topic.

- **Concentration field** — proton/ion gradients, neurotransmitter diffusion.
  Render as a dotted density. Higher density on one side of a membrane.
- **Rotary machine** — ATP synthase, bacterial flagellum. A subunit visibly
  rotates in response to flow.
- **Anchored module** — membrane-bound complexes (ETC, receptors). Hard
  rectangles or rounded shapes embedded in the membrane line.
- **Directed flow arrow** — a *causal* arrow. Never decorative.
- **State diagram** — discrete biological states (open/closed channels,
  bound/unbound enzymes). Render as nodes with transitions.
- **Comparative split** — two scenarios side by side (innate vs adaptive,
  with/without enzyme).

Forbidden metaphors: anything that implies homunculi, anything cartoonish,
anything that implies intent on the part of a molecule.
