# Misconceptions sub-prompt

Augments concept extraction. The point of misconceptions is not to mock the
learner — it is to identify the wrong internal model that the learner is
*statistically likely* to construct from a textbook reading, and to give the
correct internal model that replaces it.

For each misconception:

- **wrong**: state the misconception in the learner's voice. It should sound
  reasonable; that is *why* it sticks.
- **right**: state the correct mechanism in one sentence.
- **why_it_sticks**: 1–2 sentences on the deeper reason the wrong model is
  attractive (e.g., it satisfies a teleological intuition, or it conflates
  two scales).

Quality bar: at least one misconception must be about *energy* or
*information flow*, not labels.
